Statement of Compliance under the Information Technology (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules, 2021

 

Trilo (hereinafter “Trilo”) operates as a social media application. Trilo is classified as a Social Media Intermediary as defined under the Information Technology (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules, 2021 (hereinafter the “Rules”) and has implemented the following policies and procedures to comply with the requirements of the Rules.

 

Trilo’s Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, and Community Guidelines (collectively “Trilo Policies”) are available within the app in the Profile tab. Trilo informs users of any changes in the Policies at least once a calendar year. Additionally, Trilo notifies users at least once annually that non-compliance with the Policies may result in immediate termination of access or removal of non-compliant information, or both. Such notifications are provided via push notifications in the Important Notification tab.

 

In compliance with the Rules, Trilo Policies specifically prohibit users from publishing content that:

(i) invades another’s bodily privacy or is harmful or harassing based on gender, especially with regard to children;

(ii) is patently false or misleading, while appearing as fact;

(iii) impersonates, deceives, or is false with the intention of causing harm, wrongful loss, harassment, wrongful gain, or fraud;

(iv) threatens the unity, integrity, defense, security, or sovereignty of India; or

(v) violates any law.

 

Trilo strives to deploy technology-based measures, including automated tools or other mechanisms, to proactively identify and remove content that falls within the above categories. Trilo enforces a strict No Nudity Policy, and any content or interactions containing nudity will be immediately removed or terminated as necessary.

 

Orders by a court of competent jurisdiction, or from an Appropriate Government or its agency for takedown or other purposes, should be addressed to the Resident Grievance Officer via the email  jiva6514@gmail.com or via this link.

 

Trilo will remove or disable access to any stored, hosted, or published information within 36 hours of receiving a court order or notification from the Government or its authorized agencies, stating that such information is unlawful or prohibited by law in relation to the interest of the sovereignty and integrity of India, security of the State, etc., as stated in the Rules.

 

Upon receipt of a written order from a lawfully authorized government agency clearly stating the purpose, Trilo will provide any requested information or assistance within its control or possession to the government authority within 72 hours.

 

In compliance with the Rules, (i) registration information of a user shall be retained by Trilo for a period of 180 days from the date of cancellation or withdrawal of registration; and (ii) information that has been removed or access to which has been disabled shall be retained for 180 days for investigation purposes, or for such longer periods as may be required by the Court or authorized Government Agencies.

 

Trilo’s mechanism for dealing with grievances is detailed in the section titled Trilo Process for Grievance Redressal.

 

Upon receipt of a court order or order from a competent authority as specified under the Rules, Trilo may enable the identification of

 

the first originator of any information deemed necessary under the applicable laws and regulations.
